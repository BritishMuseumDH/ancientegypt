About the Object 
 

Beit el-Wali Comes to the Museum

Joseph Bonomi made moulds of many of the ancient sculptures and wall reliefs that he saw on his journeys through Egypt and Nubia. When he returned to England, he made plaster casts from these moulds. When Bonomi died, he left his collection of casts to the British Museum.

The museum originally displayed many casts in the galleries, but today most of the objects that you see in the galleries are real. In fact, the Beit el-Wali cast is the only cast from the Egyptian Antiquities collection that is still on display.
