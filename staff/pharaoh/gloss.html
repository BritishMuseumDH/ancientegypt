A list of complicated words, Egyptological terms and historical terms that are used and defined in the site.

Cast
A model of an object that is made from pouring plaster or metal into a mould.

Nubia
A term used to refer to the land that lay directly south of ancient Egypt. Today the northern part of ancient Nubia lies within the borders of modern Egypt, and the southern part within the borders of modern Sudan.

Pharaoh
Pharaoh' is the term we use today to describe the rulers of ancient Egypt. 'Pharaoh' is actually a Greek word that is based on an Egyptian word that meant 'great house'. When this word was first used, it referred to the palace and its greatness, not just to the ruler himself. However, later in Egyptian history the title 'pharaoh' meant ruler.

Relief
The word used to describe scenes, symbols and figures that are carved onto a wall or slab of rock. Sunken relief is when the main aspects of the picture are carved into the rock (or other hard surface). Raised relief is when the background of the scene is carved out so that the main figures and symbols stick out from the wall.

Tribute
Raw materials (like gold), or things that were made (like gold bracelets), that were given as a 'gift' by a country that was under the control of another country to the ruler of the controlling country.

Exotic
Something that is unusual or mysterious - often from a foreign land.

Afterlife
The Egyptians believed that, when an ordinary person died, they passed through the underworld where they would be judged. If they they were judged to be pure and good, they would go to a place that was very much like Egypt, and live there forever. The Egyptians believed that when a pharaoh died, he went to live with his 'father' the sun god Amun-Ra.

Apis Bull
A sacred animal to the ancient Egyptians whose cult was centred around Memphis in Lower Egypt. It was very rare for a black bull to be born with white markings, so when one was, the Egyptians saw it as a sacred sign and worshipped the animal. One bull at a time with the white markings was named the 'Apis Bull'. The Apis Bull was honoured in ceremonies and then mummified and buried in a tomb when it died. The mother of the Apis Bull was called the 'Isis Cow' and was also worshipped.

Appointed
To be named to a post by the pharaoh or an important official.

Archer
A person who shoots a bow and arrow.

Asiatic
A term sometimes used to describe people who came from the ancient Levant (an old term used to describe the area of the Middle East covering the modern states of: Israel, Jordan, Lebanon and Syria).

Aswan High Dam
A dam built in the 1960s to control the annual flooding of the Nile, and to create a reservoir. The water flowing from the Nile sources collects south of the dam forming Lake Nasser which covers over with water much of the area that was ancient Nubia.

Barge
A large boat, also called a 'bark' or 'barque'.

Bronze
A metal made from mixing copper and tin.

Cartouche
The oval-shaped symbol in which the names of kings and queens of Egypt were written.

Cataract
Rocky areas in the river bed of the Nile which make the water flow unevenly (rapids). There are six 'cataracts' on the Nile between Aswan and Khartoum.

Cobra
A deadly snake that used to be common in Egypt. Before it strikes, it raises up its head and shows its fangs. It was the symbol of Wadjyt, the goddess of Lower Egypt.

Composite
Something that is made from a combination of materials.

Copper
An orange-brown coloured metal.

Egyptologist
A person who studies ancient Egypt. This term was used a lot in the 19th century to describe people who were very interested in ancient Egypt. Today, 'Egyptologist' is most often used to describe people who go to university to learn about ancient Egypt.

Exotic
Something that is unusual or mysterious - often from a foreign land.

Flint
A hard stone, used to make arrowheads and stone tools.

Harmony
When something is well-balanced and working well.

Hide
The skin of an animal, including the fur.

Hieroglyphic/ Hieroglyphs
Hieroglyphic writing was a form of writing used by the ancient Egyptians. The individual characters are called ‘hieroglyphs'

Hittites
A powerful empire that was based in the area that is now Turkey. The Hittites were powerful in about 1300-1200 B.C., and struggled with the Egyptians for control in the Levant (an old term used to describe the area of the Middle East covering the modern states of: Israel, Jordan, Lebanon and Syria).

Imported
Something which has been grown (or made) in one country, but is brought into another country and sold there. For instance, the Egyptians did not have much timber in their country, so they imported it from places where there were tall trees.

Lake Nasser
The reservoir that was formed after the Nile was blocked by the Aswan High Dam. Lake Nasser covers much of the area that was ancient Nubia.

Levant
The name used to describe an area of the Middle East covering the modern states of: Israel, Jordan, Lebanon and Syria.

Lotus
A plant also known as a 'water lily' that lives in the water. The flower emerges from the water to bloom in the air. The Egyptians saw the lotus flower as a symbol of rebirth. They believed that at the beginning of time the young sun-god had come out from the water.

Mace
The mace was a weapon shaped like a club that was commonly used in ancient Egypt from the Predynastic period.

Mould
A mould is made from pressing an object into wet plaster or soft wax so that the object's shape, texture, and decorations make an impression into the plaster or wax. A copy (cast) of the object can then be made by pouring a material that will harden (like plaster) into the mould.

Natural Resources
Something that is easily available to the people who live in an area, rather than something that needs to be brought into the area from another place. For instance, tall trees were not one of Egypt's natural resources, so they needed to import trees from a country where there were many forests, like Phoenicia (modern-day Lebanon).

Near East
A vague name used to describe the countries to the northeast of the Egyptian border. This area includes the Levant and land directly to the east of it.The Levant is the name used to describe an area of the Middle East covering the modern states of: Israel, Jordan, Lebanon and Syria.

Oblong
A shape that looks like a flattened circle.

Offering
Something that is offered to the gods. Offerings could be food, valuables, special oils, or animals to be sacrificed.

Papyrus
Papyrus was a reed that grew in ancient Egypt that was used in many different ways. It was cut into strips, woven together, pounded, and dried to make sheets which were written on. Papyrus was also gathered, tied together in bunches, and used to make small fishing boats. 'Papyri' is the plural form of the word papyrus.

Phoenicia
The Greek name for the country that existed in the first and second millenia B.C. located where Lebanon is today. The Phoenicians are known today as the inventors of the alphabet, and as excellent shipbuilders, sailors, and navigators.

Precious
Something that is very special or valuable.

Regalia
The ceremonial dress and objects that were carried by the pharaoh to show that he was the ruler.

Reign
The period during which a king or queen (or pharaoh) rules a country.

Sceptre
The symbol of kingship that would be carried by the pharaoh.

Scribe
An ancient Egyptian man who learned to read and write hieroglyphs and hieratic (a quicker and shorter form of hieroglyphs). Scribes had two main duties, first, they were the only people in ancient Egypt who could read and write the sacred hieroglyphs that would appear on temples and in tombs, and second, they kept government records and wrote letters for the pharaoh.

Script
A group of signs and letters used to make words.

Sinew
The tendons (muscles) of an animal.

Staff
A long stick made of either wood or metal that is carried by a person to show their position in the government or the priesthood.

Succeed
To become the next ruler. For instance, Ramesses II succeeded his father as pharaoh.

Suspicious
Wary; to be unsure of someone or something because you think that they might try to do you some harm.

Symbol
An image which represents an idea or belief.

Symbolic
An image which is used to communicate an idea or belief.

Taxes
A percentage of a person's yearly income or crops that was collected by the pharaoh.

Trade
The exchange of goods for money, or for goods of equal value.

Tribute
Raw materials (like gold), or things that were made (like gold bracelets), that were given as a 'gift' by a country that was under the control of another country to the ruler of the controlling country.

Tunic
A type of long shirt.

Unified
To be brought together; to become one.
