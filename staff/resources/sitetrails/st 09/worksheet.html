The Scribe's Tools

 

Scribes were the only people in ancient Egypt who knew how to read and write. This was considered a great privilege, and scribes were respected by most people.

Scribes had many different tools which they used to do their job. Today you will follow a scribe around and look after his tools for him. Pay attention, for if you leave something behind, the scribe will not be able to do his work.

You hold out the briefcase to him and he looks through for his palette. He pulls the palette out of the briefcase and puts it under his arm. Describe what the palette looks like, then sketch it below.





The palette is the most important tool that the scribe uses. He adds water to the ink on the end of the palette and pulls out one of the reed pens. Scribes only needed two colours for record-keeping. Most of the writing was done in black ink, but each sentence began with a letter in red ink. Red ink was also used to make corrections. Other colours of ink or paint were used to decorate the scenes drawn on papyrus. Look at the end of the palette where there are still traces of paint.
