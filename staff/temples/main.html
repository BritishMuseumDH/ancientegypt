The temples chapter offers students the opportunity of exploring a reconstruction of an ancient Egyptian temple. The ideas and beliefs associated with temples, as well as temple design and use, are explained in the context of the building.


The home page is an introduction to the decoration and purpose of ancient Egyptian temples.


The story presents each room of the temple as though the user was walking through the temple. Explanations are provided for specific architectural or structural elements. This section could be used to discuss the ways that the design and use of structures can reflect spiritual or religious ideas and beliefs.


The explore section is centred around a cut-away model of an ancient Egyptian temple. Pupils can decide which rooms they would like to find out about, and explore them in whatever order they wish. Within each room are clickable statues, architectural elements, and wall reliefs. This section could be used to discuss the ways that the design and use of structures can reflect spiritual or religious ideas and beliefs. This section could also be used to discuss the importance of statues, architectural elements and wall reliefs in ancient Egyptian temples.


The challenge introduces the number system used by the ancient Egyptians in the context of an ancient Egyptian temple. The activity teaches the basic principles of ancient Egyptian numbers, while presenting the idea of the temple as an centre of economic activity in an ancient Egyptian town.
